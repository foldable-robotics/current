    A02: Kinematics

    Introduction

    The purpose of this assignment is to demonstrate your knowledge of kinematics through exemplar exercises, specifically the following content

    • Vectors and vector operations
    • The use of vectors in describing constraints
    • The creation of vector-based expressions to describe key aspects of robotic systems such as the Jacobian and to demonstrate its use
    • Key uses of the quaternion in hinged and foldable systems

    Instructions

    Part 1

    \[\begin{align} \vec{v}_1=&c_1 \hat{a}_x+c_2 \hat{a}_y+c_3 \hat{a}_z\\ % \vec{v}_2=&c_4 \hat{a}_x+c_5 \hat{a}_y+c_6 \hat{a}_z\\ \vec{v}_2=&c_4 \hat{b}_x+c_5 \hat{b}_y+c_6\hat{b}_z \end{align}\]
    1. Write the expanded form of $\vec{v}_1 \cdot \vec{v}_2 $. Do not just show your answers or skip steps, show your process of expanding and simplifying each expression. You can do this by hand or in latex.

    2. Now assume you are given more information from question 1 about the relationship between the \(A\) and \(B\) reference frames. Simplify your expression for problem 3 using \(\theta=\frac{\pi}{3}\) and

      \[\sideset{^{a}}{^{b}}R (\theta) = \begin{array}{c|ccc}& b_x& b_y& b_z\\ \hline a_x &\cos{\theta} &0& \sin{\theta} \\ a_y &0&1&0\\ a_z & -\sin{\theta} & 0& \cos{\theta} \end{array} \]

    3. Write an equation that constrains \(\vec{w}\) to be \(l\) units away from the line that includes \(\vec{u}\) and \(\vec{v}\).

    4. Regarding question 3

      1. how many degrees of freedom does vector \(\vec{w}\) have?
      2. What joint type is this equivalent to?

    Part 2

    Given the following robot:

    1. Write the two incremental rotation tables, $\sideset{^{a}}{^{b}}R (\theta_1) $ and $\sideset{^{b}}{^{c}}R (\theta_2) $.
    2. Find the vector \(\vec{r}\), describing the end effector's position in terms of each link's "native" unit vectors.
    3. Express \(\vec{r}\) into base frame components (\(\hat{a}_x\), \(\hat{a}_y\), and \(\hat{a}_z\)).

    4. Determine the Jacobian, \(J\), for the end effector by finding \(\dot{y} = \left.\frac{d\vec{r}}{dt}\right|_A\) in terms of \(\dot{\theta}=\begin{bmatrix}\dot{\theta}_1 \\ \dot{\theta}_2\end{bmatrix}\), such that (at \(\theta_1=\frac{\pi}{6}\),\(\theta_2=\frac{\pi}{6},l_b=1,l_c=1\)),

      \[\dot{y} = J\dot{\theta}\]

      You may use either a numerical or symbolic approach

    5. Now say the robot's joints move at a rate of \(\dot{{\theta}} = \begin{bmatrix}2.2\\3.1\end{bmatrix}\). What is the velocity of the end-effector (in the same configuration as the previous problem)?

    6. Now say a force of \(\vec{f}=500\hat{a}_x+1.7\hat{a}_y+3.2\hat{a}_z\) N is exerted on the end-effector, with \(\theta_1=\frac{\pi}{2}\),\(\theta_2=0,l_b=1,l_c=1\). What torques are required at each joint to hold the robot in static equilibrium?
    7. Please explain why, given a force of 500N in the x direction, the torques at the joints are so low.
